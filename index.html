üåç 2. Scientific Background: The Living Monument

The magnificent Baobab is scientifically known as Adansonia digitata. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which also includes hibiscus and cotton. These trees are titans of the plant world; they can soar to heights of over 25 meters (82 feet) but are most famous for their colossal, swollen trunks, which can reach 11 meters in diameter.

Their lifespan is staggering, with the oldest verified specimens living for well over a millennium, making them living relics. While most famously associated with the African savanna, different species are also found in Madagascar and Australia.

Their unique shape is a direct result of their **adaptation to harsh climates**. They are xerophytes, meaning they are specialized to thrive in environments with very little water.

  • üåø **Soil Health:** Its extensive root system helps stabilize the soil, actively working to **prevent desertification and erosion** across the savanna landscape.
  • üåø **Nutrient Cycling:** Falling leaves and organic matter enrich the often-poor savanna soils.
